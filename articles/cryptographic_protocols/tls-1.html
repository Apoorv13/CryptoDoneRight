TLSv1.0 is a revision to the SSLv3 and was released in January of 1999. Just like previous revisions to SSLv3, this revision aims at improving the security of the protocol by improving the encryption and hashing standards. These revisions can be however ignored given the current context of TLS. There have been vulnerabilites found in TLSv1.0 and PCI has advised that organizations move away from TLSv1.0 to TLSv1.1 or higher by June 30, 2018.

Attacks:
BEAST: The attack that killed off TLSv1.0(and all earlier versions). BEAST allows attackers to decrypt data through attacks such as MITM. Learn more.
